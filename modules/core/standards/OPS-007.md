# OPS-007: Support model and on-call defined

## Standard

Every live service MUST have a documented support model defining: who is responsible during business hours, escalation path, out-of-hours coverage (if applicable), and response time targets per severity.

## Rationale

- Without a defined support model, incidents fall through the cracks or are handled by whoever happens to notice
- Clear responsibility reduces mean time to acknowledge (MTTA) and mean time to resolve (MTTR)
- Escalation paths prevent single individuals from being overwhelmed during major incidents
- Response time targets set expectations with stakeholders and users
- GDS Service Standard requires services to demonstrate they can be operated and supported before going live

## What good looks like

- Support model documented in the service's operational runbook (not just in someone's head)
- Clear definition of who is on-call and when — rota published and visible to the team
- Severity levels defined with corresponding response time targets:
  - P1 (Critical): service down for all users — acknowledge within 15 minutes, resolve/mitigate within 1 hour
  - P2 (High): major feature unavailable — acknowledge within 30 minutes, resolve within 4 hours
  - P3 (Medium): degraded performance — acknowledge within 2 hours, resolve within 1 business day
  - P4 (Low): minor issue — acknowledge within 1 business day, resolve within 5 business days
- Escalation path documented: first responder, team lead, service owner, senior management
- On-call compensation and toil budget agreed with the team
- Regular rotation to prevent burnout (minimum 2 people in rota, maximum 1 week per rotation)
- Handover process between shifts/rotations documented
- Support model reviewed and updated at least quarterly

**Support model template (for team runbook):**

```markdown
## Support Model — [Service Name]

### Business hours support (Mon-Fri 09:00-17:30)
- **Primary responder**: Rotating weekly (see PagerDuty/Opsgenie rota)
- **Secondary responder**: Previous week's primary
- **Escalation**: Team Lead → Delivery Manager → Service Owner

### Out-of-hours (if applicable)
- **Coverage**: [24/7 | Best-effort | None — state which]
- **On-call rota**: [Link to PagerDuty/Opsgenie schedule]
- **Compensation**: [TOIL / on-call allowance per team agreement]

### Severity definitions and response targets

| Severity | Definition | Acknowledge | Resolve/Mitigate |
|----------|-----------|-------------|------------------|
| P1 | Service down for all users | 15 min | 1 hour |
| P2 | Major feature unavailable | 30 min | 4 hours |
| P3 | Degraded performance | 2 hours | 1 business day |
| P4 | Minor issue | 1 business day | 5 business days |

### Escalation path
1. Primary on-call (PagerDuty alert)
2. Secondary on-call (auto-escalation after 10 min)
3. Team Lead (after 30 min or P1 declared)
4. Service Owner / Delivery Manager (major incident)

### Review schedule
- Rota reviewed: monthly
- Support model reviewed: quarterly
- Last review: [date]
```

## What bad looks like

- No documented support model — team relies on tribal knowledge
- Single point of failure — one person always handles incidents
- No severity definitions — everything treated as urgent or everything ignored
- On-call rota exists but is ignored or has no escalation
- Response time targets not defined — stakeholders have no expectations to hold the team to
- Support model written at launch and never updated as the team changes

## Enforcement

| Mechanism | What is checked | When |
|-----------|----------------|------|
| **Periodic audit** | Support model document exists and is current; on-call rota is populated and being followed | Service assessment (beta/live) and quarterly operational review |
| **Ways of working** | Team reviews and updates support model; rota rotation functioning | Ongoing — documented in team charter and runbook |

**Primary enforcement: Periodic audit + Ways of working.** Service assessments verify a support model exists before live deployment. Quarterly operational reviews confirm the model is current and being followed.
