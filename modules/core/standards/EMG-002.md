# EMG-002: AI systems have a transparency statement

## Standard

Services that use AI to make or support decisions affecting users SHOULD publish a transparency statement explaining: what AI is used for, how it influences outcomes, what data it was trained on, how to challenge a decision, and known limitations.

## Rationale

- Public trust in government depends on transparency
- Algorithmic decision-making without explanation creates accountability gaps
- Aligns with CDDO AI framework

## What good looks like

- Published transparency statement linked from the service footer and/or decision notification page
- Plain-English explanation of AI's role — e.g. "We use a language model to draft reply letters; a caseworker reviews every letter before it is sent"
- Explanation of human oversight — who reviews AI outputs, what authority they have to override
- Process for challenging AI-influenced decisions — clear route (e.g. "Contact us to request a human review of this decision")
- Statement updated when the AI system changes (new model version, different training data, expanded scope)
- Statement follows the Algorithmic Transparency Recording Standard (ATRS) format where applicable

## Enforcement

| Mechanism | What is checked | When |
|-----------|----------------|------|
| **Periodic audit** | Reviewed at service assessment for any service using AI in decision support | Service assessment |

**Primary enforcement: Periodic audit.** Reviewed at service assessment for any service using AI in decision support.

## Quick reference

**Algorithmic Transparency Recording Standard (ATRS) template:**
https://www.gov.uk/government/collections/algorithmic-transparency-recording-standard-hub

**What the transparency statement must include:**
- Name and description of the AI/algorithmic tool
- What decisions or outputs it supports
- How the AI influences the final outcome (advisory, automated, hybrid)
- What data the model was trained on (high-level summary)
- Known limitations and failure modes
- Human oversight arrangements (who reviews, escalation path)
- How users can challenge an AI-influenced decision
- Date of last review and next scheduled review
- Contact details for questions about the AI system

**Where to publish:**
Link the statement from the service itself (e.g. footer link "How we use AI in this service") and register it on the ATRS hub.
