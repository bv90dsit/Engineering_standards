# ENG-007: Code review turnaround within 24 hours

## Standard

Pull requests SHOULD receive initial review within 24 business hours. Reviews blocking deployment SHOULD be prioritised. Teams SHOULD agree review norms in their team charter.

## Rationale

- DORA research shows that lead time for changes is a key predictor of software delivery performance — code review is often the largest contributor to lead time
- Long review cycles cause context-switching for authors (returning to a PR days later) and increase merge conflict risk
- Blocked PRs encourage large, infrequent deployments which increase change failure rate
- Quick review turnaround creates a culture of collaboration and shared ownership
- Google's engineering research found that optimal PR throughput occurs when reviews happen within 24 hours

## What good looks like

- Initial review (approve, request changes, or meaningful comment) within 24 business hours of PR being opened
- Team has documented review norms in their team charter:
  - Who reviews (round-robin, codeowners, voluntary)
  - Expected turnaround time
  - What constitutes a "blocking" vs "non-blocking" comment
  - When to approve with suggestions vs request changes
- PR authors make reviews easy: small PRs (< 400 lines changed), clear descriptions, linked tickets
- Reviewers prioritise unblocking others over starting new work — "review before write" culture
- Stale PRs (> 48 hours without review) flagged automatically and discussed at standup
- Teams track review turnaround time and discuss trends at retrospectives

**Team charter clause (copy-paste):**

```markdown
## Code Review Norms

### Turnaround
- Initial review within 24 business hours (aim for same day)
- If you cannot review within 24h, reassign or comment explaining delay

### Review priorities
1. PRs blocking deployment or other team members
2. Small PRs (< 100 lines) — quick wins, review immediately
3. Larger PRs — schedule focused review time

### Commenting conventions
- **Blocking** (must fix before merge): prefix with `blocker:`
- **Non-blocking** (suggestion, take it or leave it): prefix with `nit:` or `suggestion:`
- **Question** (need clarification): prefix with `question:`

### PR author responsibilities
- Keep PRs small (< 400 lines changed where possible)
- Write a clear description linking to the ticket/story
- Self-review before requesting others
- Respond to review comments within 24 business hours
```

## What bad looks like

- PRs sitting for days without any review — authors waiting, context lost
- No agreed review norms — some PRs reviewed instantly, others ignored for a week
- Reviews used as gatekeeping rather than collaboration
- Huge PRs (1000+ lines) that no one wants to review, creating a vicious cycle
- "LGTM" reviews with no meaningful engagement
- PRs approved but not merged for days — deployment blocked by process not people
- Authors not responding to review comments, creating long back-and-forth cycles

## Enforcement

| Mechanism | What is checked | When |
|-----------|----------------|------|
| **Ways of working** | Team has documented review norms; review turnaround discussed at retros; stale PRs flagged at standup | Ongoing — documented in team charter |

**Primary enforcement: Ways of working.** This is a team practice standard enforced through team agreements and cultural norms. Teams document their review expectations in their charter and hold each other accountable through standups and retrospectives.
