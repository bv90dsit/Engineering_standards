# OPS-001: Monitor and alert on service health

## Standard

All production services MUST have monitoring and alerting in place. Teams MUST define Service Level Indicators (SLIs) and Service Level Objectives (SLOs). Alerts MUST route to a responsible team within business hours at minimum.

## Rationale

- You cannot fix what you cannot see
- Government services have a duty of care to users — downtime has real-world impact
- Proactive alerting reduces mean time to recovery (MTTR)
- SLOs provide an objective measure of service quality

## What good looks like

- Dashboard showing key SLIs: availability, latency (p50, p95, p99), error rate
- SLOs defined and published (e.g. 99.9% availability, p95 latency < 500ms)
- Alerts fire before users notice (error budget burn rate, not just threshold breaches)
- Runbooks exist for every alert — what to check, who to escalate to
- On-call or named support rota during service hours
- Structured logging with correlation IDs for request tracing
- Quarterly review of alert quality (noisy alerts get fixed or removed)

## What bad looks like

- No monitoring — team finds out about outages from Twitter/users
- Dashboard exists but no one looks at it
- Alerts that fire constantly and are ignored (alert fatigue)
- No runbooks — responders guess what to do
- "The contractor who set up monitoring left 2 years ago"

## Enforcement

| Mechanism | What is checked | When |
|-----------|----------------|------|
| **Periodic audit** | SLOs defined and published, dashboards exist, alert routing configured, runbooks present | Service assessment (alpha → beta → live) and quarterly reviews |
| **Ways of working** | On-call rota maintained, alert quality reviewed, incidents trigger blameless post-mortems | Ongoing — documented in team charter |
| **Automated (CI/CD)** | Monitoring-as-code files exist (e.g. Terraform alerting rules, Grafana dashboards in repo) | PR check — heuristic only; presence ≠ quality |

**Primary enforcement: Periodic audit.** This standard cannot be meaningfully validated by a pipeline. The compliance checker flags it for manual review at service assessment.
