# EMG-004: AI training data checked for bias and IP compliance

## Standard

Teams deploying or fine-tuning AI models SHOULD document the training data provenance and assess it for bias and intellectual property compliance. Models trained on user data MUST have appropriate consent and data governance.

## Rationale

- Biased training data produces discriminatory outcomes in public services
- IP-infringing training data creates legal liability
- Government must demonstrate fairness

## What good looks like

- Training data documented with a data card — source, consent basis, licensing, date range, demographic coverage
- Bias assessment performed pre-deployment using a structured checklist (see Quick reference below)
- Fairness metrics defined and monitored — e.g. demographic parity, equalised odds, predictive parity across protected characteristics
- Data governance board sign-off for personal data — DPIA completed, lawful basis confirmed, retention schedule set
- Regular review of model outputs for disparate impact — monthly disaggregated analysis by ethnicity, age, sex, disability status
- IP compliance verified — no copyrighted material used without licence; open-data sources preferred

## Enforcement

| Mechanism | What is checked | When |
|-----------|----------------|------|
| **Periodic audit** | Bias assessment and data provenance reviewed at service assessment for AI-dependent services | Service assessment |

**Primary enforcement: Periodic audit.** Bias assessment and data provenance reviewed at service assessment for AI-dependent services.

## Quick reference

**Bias assessment checklist:**
- [ ] Training data demographic breakdown documented and compared to target population
- [ ] Known gaps in representation identified (e.g. under-represented regions, languages, age groups)
- [ ] Proxy variables for protected characteristics identified and mitigated
- [ ] Model outputs tested for disparate impact across protected characteristics (Equality Act 2010)
- [ ] Historical bias in source data acknowledged and corrective measures documented
- [ ] Third-party data suppliers assessed for their own bias and quality controls

**Fairness metrics examples:**
| Metric | Definition | Threshold |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Demographic parity | P(positive outcome) is equal across groups | Ratio >= 0.8 (four-fifths rule) |
| Equalised odds | True positive and false positive rates equal across groups | Difference < 0.05 |
| Predictive parity | Precision equal across groups | Difference < 0.05 |
| Counterfactual fairness | Outcome unchanged when protected attribute is flipped | Pass/Fail |
